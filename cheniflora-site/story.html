Started on a hostel desk, one pipe cleaner at a time

"I made my first chenille flower during a boring lecture in second year — by the time the class ended I'd made six and given away four."

That's the honest version. No business plan, no mission statement — just a box of pipe cleaners from a stationery shop near campus and a habit that wouldn't stop. The first "order" was a friend who insisted on paying ₹20 for a flower I was going to give her anyway.

Three years on, Cheniflora is still small on purpose. Every stem is still twisted by hand, still checked one by one before it's wrapped. The ₹40 starting price isn't a discount strategy — it's what the first flower actually cost to make, and we never saw a reason to move far from that.

How It's Made

Six steps, the same way every time

01

Material Selection

Chenille stems sorted by colour and thickness before anything is cut.

02

Crafting

Each petal is shaped and twisted around floral wire by hand.

03

Assembly

Petals are bound into the full flower head, then stemmed and leafed.

04

Quality Check

Every piece is checked for loose threads or uneven petals before packing.

05

Packaging

Wrapped in cellophane or netting, ribbon-tied, message card added.

06

Delivery

Boxed flat to protect the petals, shipped within 2–3 days of order.

What We Believe

The values behind the price tag

Affordable doesn't mean cheap

₹40 is a real price for real handmade work — it's possible because we keep things small, not because we cut corners.

Imperfect on purpose

No two flowers twist exactly the same. That's not a flaw to fix — it's the proof a person made it.

Built to keep, not to wilt

No water, no expiry date. What you give today still looks the same a year from now.

Made for everyday gifting

Not just anniversaries and weddings — graduations, thank-yous, "just because" moments too.
